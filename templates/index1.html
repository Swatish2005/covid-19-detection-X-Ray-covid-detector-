Information Center

What is COVID-19?

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Most people infected with the virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. However, some will become seriously ill and require medical attention.

Read more at WHO

Watch for Symptoms

Most Common

  • Fever
  • Dry Cough
  • Tiredness
  • Loss of taste/smell

Less Common

  • Sore throat
  • Headache
  • Aches and pains
  • Red eyes

Serious Symptoms

  • Difficulty breathing
  • Loss of speech/mobility
  • Chest pain
  • *Seek immediate medical care

Prevention Guidelines

DO's

  • • Wash your hands regularly with soap.
  • • Wear a mask in public places.
  • • Maintain social distancing (6 feet).
  • • Get vaccinated when it's your turn.

DON'Ts

  • • Don't touch your eyes, nose, or mouth.
  • • Don't visit crowded places unnecessarily.
  • • Don't ignore symptoms like fever or cough.
  • • Don't share personal items like towels.

Home Isolation Protocol

If you test positive but have mild symptoms, you may be advised to isolate at home. Stay in a separate room, use a separate bathroom if possible, and wear a mask if you must be around others.

Monitor oxygen saturation with a pulse oximeter regularly.

Vaccination

Vaccines are safe and effective at preventing serious illness. Stay up to date with your boosters to protect yourself and others.

  • Reduces risk of severe symptoms
  • Helps achieve herd immunity

Mental Wellbeing

Pandemic stress is real. Take breaks from news, connect with friends virtually, and maintain a healthy routine.

  • Practice mindfulness & meditation
  • Exercise regularly at home

Myth Busters

MYTH

"Antibiotics can cure COVID-19."

FACT

No. Antibiotics treat bacteria, not viruses. COVID-19 is caused by a virus.

MYTH

"Hot weather kills the virus."

FACT

The virus can spread in all climates, including hot and humid weather.
