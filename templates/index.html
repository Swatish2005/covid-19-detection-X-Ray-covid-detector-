About COVID-19

Essential information about prevention, symptoms, and what to do

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.

How to Avoid COVID-19

  • Get vaccinated and boosted
  • Wear masks in crowded places
  • Maintain social distancing
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Improve indoor ventilation
  • Avoid touching face
  • Clean high-touch surfaces

Common Symptoms

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Headache
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

If You Have COVID-19

  • Isolate for at least 5 days
  • Monitor your symptoms
  • Stay hydrated and rest
  • Use over-the-counter medications
  • Seek medical help if symptoms worsen
  • Use separate bathroom if possible
  • Wear mask around others

What Not To Do

  • Don't ignore severe symptoms
  • Don't take unproven treatments
  • Don't break isolation early
  • Don't share personal items
  • Don't panic - most cases are mild
  • Don't use public transport while sick
  • Don't stop medications without consulting doctor

If You Don't Have COVID

  • Continue preventive measures
  • Get tested if exposed
  • Keep up with vaccinations
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle
  • Have a plan if you get sick
  • Stock essential supplies
  • Know your local testing centers

Post-COVID Prevention

After recovering from COVID-19, you should:

  • Gradually return to normal activities
  • Watch for post-COVID conditions (fatigue, brain fog, etc.)
  • Continue following public health guidelines
  • Consider donating plasma if eligible
  • Get medical follow-up if needed
  • Practice breathing exercises if lung affected
  • Monitor for blood clotting symptoms

Remember: Even after recovery, you can get COVID-19 again. Continue taking precautions to protect yourself and others.

Official Resources & Emergency Contacts

MOHFW Website WHO COVID-19 MOHFW Helpline: 1075 National Emergency: 112 Medical Emergency: 108

When to Seek Emergency Care:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent chest pain or pressure
  • New confusion
  • Inability to wake or stay awake
  • Pale, gray, or blue-colored skin/lips
